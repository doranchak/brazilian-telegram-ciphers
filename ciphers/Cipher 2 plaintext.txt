Jarls' notes:

The first thing one can do when a transposition cipher is suspected is to carry 
out a simple periodical n-gram repeat analysis:

1) Put the cipher in 2 columns (period 2) and read the text vertically while 
counting the number of bigram or trigram repeats. Compare the number of repeats 
to shuffles and calculate the odds.

2) Repeat step 1 for 3 columns, 4 columns, 5 columns (period 3, 4, 5) etc up to 
some meaningful limit (cipher length / 2 for example).

3) If one find odds that are greater than what are expected to find after such 
an analysis then the cipher may be transposed in exactly this way. In case of 
the Portuguese cipher the odds at 15 columns were (very roughly) a 9 sigma 
observation for trigram repeats.

4) Reading the cipher by columns vertically in a 15 by 22 configuration thus 
revealed the following text:

ENCONTRO DE SEJVS SINE EROS CHEFES REPUBLICA NAS A
CATAR VOSSA SPINIAO ELEICAO SCHMIDT ELLE TER A VOTACAO
HONROS A MAS HA DIFFIOULDADES SERIAS IMPOSSIBILITAM
ELEICAO DUVIDS CHEFES MUNICIPIOS QE E VOTARAM E ONVENCA
A EG NESS BEM E ONVERSA DOS PODERA O CEDER PARA ISTO
FALTA TEM PA E NINGUEM AQUI CONSEGUE A DIAMENTA CECSR
OS OBTER GOVERNADORA DIAR ELEICAO PELVM EM OS GUINZE
DIAS SAUDS ROSSO O O O SO SO

5) Transcription and spacing errors may still persist and due to my poor 
knowledge of the language I cannot proceed further with the decryption. 
Diacritics were not preserved. The last +-15 letters may be filler to complete 
the rectangle.

    1. This word is a conjugation of the verb "to find". At the beginning of the 
    message, a sentence like "I wish you are well" could be used, in the sense that 
    the interlocutor is well at that moment. But it doesn't seem to dialogue with 
    the subsequent words.

    2. This is the name of an important military and politician in that context and 
    it is spelled correctly.

    3. This word is a demonstrative pronoun and is used when the object or person 
    it refers to is far from the person speaking and spatially close to the person 
    being spoken to. In the context of the message, it does not dialogue with the 
    other words.

    4. Here you didn't form a word in Portuguese, but there are some words in 
    Portuguese that have 7 letters and "ç" in the third position. Examples: façanha 
    (feat), maçante (dull), façamos (let's do), alçapão (trapdoor), caçador 
    (Hunter).

    5. From the beginning of the word it is clear that this is a parting greeting 
    (greetings - saudações). This is how the author ends other telegrams sent to 
    the same sender in that period.

    6. This is another word that doesn't make sense in the Portuguese language. 
    Usually the messages ended in "greetings", but in this case it has one more 
    word with 8 letters. It seems to me that it could be the name of the author of 
    the message "Hercilio", it would make sense if it were. (Greetings, Hercilio).
